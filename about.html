About

Introduction

My name is Bas de Reus and I am years old. I am a game developer living in Zaandam, the Netherlands. I already have years of experience in programming and making games.

At the moment I'm attending HKU for a Bachelor of Creative Media and Game Technologies, currently in year . Already have a MBO diploma for software development focused on game development.

Why I Make Games

I enjoy creating games because I want to give others the same sense of enjoyment that games have given me. This passion is why I became a game developer. I particularly enjoy working on gameplay features and systems.

I have extensive experience with Unity and C#, as shown on the home page, but I am also broadening my skills by exploring other game engines like Godot. In addition to development, I enjoy taking on leadership roles in projects. I have frequently served as a Lead Developer or Scrum Master.

Gaming

As a game developer, it's no surprise that I also enjoy playing games. Lately, I've been playing a bit less because I find making games even more fun. I've been gaming since childhood, starting with my DS and Wii. Nowadays, I primarily play on my PC and Nintendo Switch.

I tend to gravitate towards building and simulation games, like Cities: Skylines, based on my playtime. However, I also enjoy competitive games like Super Smash Bros. Ultimate and Rainbow Six Siege.

2021

First Unity game

The first Unity game I worked on was Adley's Adventure, created in collaboration with Tatum de Vries and Patrica Kuipers. We aimed to make this project as polished as possible with our knowledge at the time. While the gameplay is simple, it felt like a significant achievement back then. My contributions included developing core mechanics such as player stats, a pick-up system for collecting eggs, a key-and-door system, and the main menu.

2022

First gamejam

After completing a few more projects, I decided to organize a game jam for fun. I invited some friends and classmates to participate, challenging ourselves to create small games in just one week. For my entry, I made a simple game about fruit dodging cutlery while collecting items. This project marked my first experience working with JSON, which I used to store the player's selection.

Neon origns

Neon origns is an hybrid learning environment (HLO), this is where I truly honed my skills in game development. During this experience, I began to fully understand the tools and techniques I was using. Previously, my approach was to make things work and move on; here, I learned to make things work and improve them—whether through cleaner code, better gameplay, or enhanced visuals. Much of what I do today stems from what I learned during this time.

Second gamejam

While organizing my own game jam was a fun experience, it was a lot to manage. So, I decided to join a public game jam on itch.io: the Wanna x Yam Game Jam 2022. The theme was "Combination," which inspired me to brainstorm ideas. I landed on the concept of colors and decided to create an educational game about mixing colors. To make it engaging, I added levels and a timer to create a complete gameplay experience.

2023

XR-Lab internship

My first internship in game development was at XR-Lab, where we focused on XR projects using technologies like virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), and mixed reality (MR). I worked on two key projects: a VR karaoke game for the TV network AVROTROS, translating their show "Beste Zangers" into an immersive VR environment, and an AR reimagination of an existing app/web app for the client Gpal. This project aimed to make education and training for blue-collar workers more engaging and enjoyable.

MBO exames

In the final semester of my MBO education, the exam project was the last hurdle before earning my diploma. I assembled a powerhouse team of artists and developers to create the best possible game for this exam. We had the opportunity to work with either two real game companies or the City of Amsterdam. After the draw, we were selected to work with the City of Amsterdam, who requested an unusual project. The goal was to develop a mobile game that would allow users to explore the area around the office in Amsterdam in a fun and educational way.
